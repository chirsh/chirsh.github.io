The Great Chicago Fire

Birth of the Modern City of Chicago

Mrs. O'Leary's Cow?

The great fire is traditionally said to have been caused by Catherine O'Leary's cow kicking over a lantern in its barn on the night of October 8, 1871. The real cause was unclear, but the result was unmistakable, over the next two days fire destroyed 4 square miles of central Chicago.

The immediete aftermath of the fire was hundreds of millions of dollars of damage and a third of the city's 300,000 residents without a home. As news spread, cities across America and abroad donated hundreds of thousands of dollars and various goods to the relief effort.

Positive Change in the Aftermath

Chicago quickly rebuilt, setting the city on a new grid system and improving fire standards. In 1884 the first skyscraper in the world was constructed in Chicago and in 1893, twenty-two years after the fire, the city hosted over twenty million people for the World's Columbian Exposition.
